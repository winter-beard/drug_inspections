In America, 70% of cancer centers are reporting a shortage of Cisplatin, a platinum-based oncology drug used particularly in soft tissue cancers and flagged for pediatric use. A 2018 hospital study has shown that nearly 50% of chemotherapy patients receive a platinum drug during their cancer treatment, and some patients receive two, and even three, different platinum drugs. Cisplatin has been used since the 1970s in cancer treatment is sometimes referred to as the "penicillan of cancer drugs". Soft tissue cancers especially, breast, colon and bladder that see some of the highest impacts to mortality risk if treatments are delayed. One medical review estimated a 20% increase in mortality risk with just four weeks of delay.

About 50% of the US' Cisplatin comes from a privately-owned Indian company called Intas Pharmaceuticals, manufacturing both finished injectibles and the active ingredient.

The current shortage began three months after the FDA found massive failures at Intas' newest facility, Pharmez compound near a village called Matoda. Here, 5 million injectible drugs, many of them cancer treatments and 1 billion solid doses of pharmaceuticals are made every year.It's one of only four FDA-approved Cisplatin-producing facilities, and manufactures an estimated 50% of the US market for Cisplatin, 20% of the US' Carboplatin imports, and 50% of its Methotrexate. Part of the reason the manufacturing marketshare is so concentrated in Intas is because most companies want little to do with the dangers of the raw platinum compounds and the thin profit margins from a drug that costs less than $50 a vial compared to thousands of dollars for doses of newer, advanced immunotherapies.

A December 2022 report the FDA, found factory staff destroying quality control documents with acid on the property and revealed the company was consistently miscounting the viable compounds of the chemotherapy injectible that was present in their cultures.

The FDA's investigating officer concluded there was no way to assure the public that previous batches of the drug were at the potency advertised, and in June, 2023 the company was issued with an Import Alert, preventing them from shipping products to the US, except for critical medicines which would undergo additional quality assurance screening.


Despite the large amount of reporting on the crisis, few media outlets have mentioned the specific shortages of Cisplatin in the past, though doctors have been quoted mentionin persistent shortages.

Cisplatin, Carboplatin and Methotrexate have been in critical supply before for years, with many researchers citing a mass exodus of manufacturers from the market in the early 2010s

A delay in chemotherapy treatment of even one month has serious impacts on mortality rates especially in cases of colon, breast and bladder where a four week-delay saw up to 20% higher mortality rates.

A visualization of all the Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient manufacturers the FDA inspected in 2021 and 2023. As the graph shows, India is by far the largest exporter of vital pharmaceutical ingredients.

Despite headlines that the recent shortage is driving more reliance on China, in light of the the FDA's temporary allowance for Chinese manufacturer Qilu Pharmaceuticals to begin importing Cisplatin and Carboplatin, my analysis of FDA inspections of API manufacturing facilities shows that there has been an explosion of facilities in India in the last three years. While the number of inspected facilities remained constant in other countries over the last three years. Compared to 2021, there have been 130 more API manufacturing facilities inspected in India in 2023.

Despite the monumental impact media and experts are ascribing to this factory notice, it is interesting to look at it in the context of all FDA inspection citations, and see just how small of a fish Intas is in the greater landscape of pharmaceutical companies manufacturing chemotherapy drugs, and just how many citations the pharmaceutical giants have received.
