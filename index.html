In America, 70% of cancer centers are reporting a shortage of Cisplatin, an oncology drug used particularly in soft tissue cancers and flagged for pediatric use. However it is soft tissue cancers especially, breast, colon and bladder that see some of the highest impacts to mortality risk if treatments are delayed. One medical review estimated a 20% increase in mortality risk with just four weeks of delay.

About 50% of the US' Cisplatin comes from a privately-owned Indian company called Intas Pharmaceuticals, manufacturing both finished injectibles and the active ingredient. Its four FDA-approved facilities in Gujurat India manufacture an estimated 50% of the US market for Cisplatin, 20% of the US' Carboplatin imports, and 50% of its Methotrexate. Part of the reason the manufacturing marketshare is so concentrated in Intas is because most companies want little to do with the raw platinum compounds used to manufacture the drugs.

The current shortage began three months after the FDA found massive failures at Intas' newest facility, Pharmez compound near a village called Matoda. Here, 5 million injectible drugs, many of them cancer treatments and 1 billion solid doses of pharmaceuticals are made every year.

A December 2022 report the FDA, found factory staff destroying quality control documents with acid on the property and revealed the company was consistently miscounting the viable compounds of the chemotherapy injectible that was present in their cultures.

The FDA's investigating officer concluded there was no way to assure the public that previous batches of the drug were at the potency advertised, and in June, 2023 the company was issued with an Import Alert, preventing them from shipping products to the US, except for critical medicines which would undergo additional quality assurance screening.

Despite the large amount of reporting on the crisis, few media outlets have mentioned the specific shortages of Cisplatin in the past, though doctors have been quoted mentionin persistent shortages.

Cisplatin, Carboplatin and Methotrexate have been in critical supply before for years, with one French Oncologist finding the rate of bladder cancer recurrence increased from 16% in those treated prior to a shortage to 43% in those treated during it.

A delay in chemotherapy treatment of even one month has serious impacts on mortality rates especially in cases of colon, breast and bladder where a four week-delay saw up to 20% higher mortality rates.
